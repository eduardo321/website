sound -Every sound that you hear are vibrations going through the air, as waves of sound. When an item gets hit it vibrates, which is why we hear it. Light travels faster than the speed of sound which is why when you see lightning, you then hear it a few seconds later. Sound is measured in frequency and amplitude.

Frequency-measures how many waves there are in a second. You can measure frequency for a set time such as three seconds, but you will always say how many waves there were per second, not how many waves there are in total. The greater the frequency the greater the pitch.

Amplitude-Amplitude-this is the height of the wave. The higher the wave the louder the sound is, so the lower the height the quieter the sound is. It also tells you about how much energy is carried by a wave. If the amplitude is high then that indicates that the wave contains a lot of energy but if the wave is low then the wave contains a low amount of energy.
